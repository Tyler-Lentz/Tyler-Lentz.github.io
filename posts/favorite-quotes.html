My Favorite Quotes from Fantasy/Sci-fi

Fantasy and sci-fi, or speculative fiction, are genres which at their core are defined by their settings. It is ironic then, that the least important part of speculative fiction is generally setting. Sure, the setting of a particular book or series might be what gets you interested in it the first place (e.g. "I want to read medieval fantasy, like Game of Thrones!"), but once you've entered that world, for most people it doesn't matter how interesting the setting is if the characters and/or plot are terrible.

If setting is relatively less important, then why write speculative fiction at all? Why don't all authors just write grounded stories set in the real world, with real characters you might find in your own neighborhood. Surely these kinds of stories would provide more relevant observations about the real world and the human condition?

Well, I just said a rhetorical question in the introduction of a blog post, so that basically guarantees that the premise is competely bogus, and is probably something no sane person would ever think. But let's dive into it.

First, fantastical settings are just cool. They can greatly enhance the entertainment of a work if done well, and for those of us looking for stories that provide a sense of magic or wonder, then it isn't really a puzzle why we care about setting.

But second, and more importantly, a great setting can provide something that more literary, or grounded, fiction cannot. Fantasical,or otherwise foreign, settings can make observations about the real world that wouldn't be possible otherwise. In other words, by creating an alternate world that is slightly different than our own, the author can draw parallels and make connections that give more insight into what makes us human.

So although setting is less important than plot and character in making a good story, in speculative fiction a great setting can provide the framework to make insights that would only be possible in some other, fantastical world. So the least important part of a good story becomes the most integral part of an amazing work of fantasy.
